Visualizing with WebAR

Buying furniture online should not mean guessing whether a sofa will swallow your living room or whether a desk aligns with your window light. Syntax WebAR places true-to-scale 3D models in your real environment — directly in the browser, with no app download required.

We integrated augmented reality because scale and context matter as much as style. A product photo on a white background cannot tell you how corduroy reads under your ceiling height, or how a desk footprint interacts with a narrow study. WebAR closes that gap before checkout.

What you need to get started

Use a smartphone or tablet with a modern browser — Chrome on Android or Safari on iOS works best. Ensure you have adequate lighting in the room where you want to preview the piece. Open any Syntax product detail page and tap View in AR to launch the experience.

How a typical session works

After granting camera access, slowly pan your device across a flat floor area so the system can detect a stable surface. Once the anchor is set, the 3D model appears at real-world scale. You can walk around it, move closer to inspect materials, and listen to the built-in audio narration that highlights dimensions, weight capacity, and design details.

On-screen controls

Our AR views include a simple control pad so you can fine-tune placement without leaving the scene. Use the directional buttons to nudge the model forward, backward, or sideways. Rotation and scale controls help when a room is tight or when you want to compare alternate layouts. When you are satisfied, add the item to your cart or return to the product page for full specifications.

Campaign and marker-based AR

Beyond product previews, Syntax also offers marker-based experiences. Scan our Summer 2026 campaign poster to explore the collection story in AR, or use the printed target images linked from individual product pages for image-tracking placement when room scanning is limited.

Tips for the best results

Clear clutter from the floor area, avoid glossy surfaces that confuse depth sensors, and preview at the same time of day you most use the room — natural light changes how fabrics and wood tones appear. If a model drifts, re-scan the floor and re-anchor. WebAR is a planning tool: the better your environment conditions, the more confident your purchase decision.
